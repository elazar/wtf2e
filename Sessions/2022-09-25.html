Summary

Dade, Clara, and Paca meet with Gregory and learn more about Sister Cathwulf. The Pack gathers with Wren at HQ, and they all share many secrets with each other. Wren senses the death of LeShaun White and is cast out of the Cadillac Company.

Notes

  • It's Sunday evening.
  • Dade discovers that it's impossible to treat the sort of long-term Lunacy that Miguel has. Practically, the best non-supernatural thing to do is to treat it like PTSD. In supernatural terms, bringing him to a peaceful place in the Hisil may have a positive effect. Dade resolves to pass this information along to Paca when the opportunity presents itself.
  • Dade, Clara, and Paca travel to the church of Lupus et Fidelis to consult with the Pack's mole in the church, Gregory. Aside from a change in the color of his robes, he looks the same. Dade gestures to lead him into an alley near the church.
    • He managed to find and get inside Sister Cathwulf's residence. It's a dive bar called "The Last Firstborn" (with a faded first "r") near the Cadillac Company's territory. The outside is free of glass and metal. The inside is guarded by a rather large bouncer. The patrons seemed antagonized.
    • Gregory managed to get into the bar's basement, which serves as Sister Cathwulf bedroom. It's surrounded by what appear to be holy books, and there's a podium nearby. There's an adjacent room that he wasn't able to enter.
    • For the most part, Sister Cathwulf goes between this bar and the church. Beyond that, her travels and calendar are rather mundane in nature. Gregory noticed her carrying a few vials on her person at some point, but couldn't ascertain what was in them.
  • Paca asks Gregory if he has a phone, but he indicates that they aren't allowed at worship and he left his at home. She provides him with her phone number and demands that he provide her with any contact information he has for members of the cult.
  • Dade urges him to attempt to discover more information about Sister Cathwulf: the nature of the vials she carries, the purpose of the room adjacent to her own, etc. Gregory suspects that the Pack will reveal themselves to the cult and asks when they intend to do so. Dade responds that they must learn the Sister's true nature first.
  • Dade texts Wren to check in, and they agree to meet with the Pack at HQ to chat.
  • Once the Pack arrives at HQ, Dade asks about the last time they heard from Wren, saying that they didn't seem themself at the time. Wren indicates they are aware of the cause of their split personalities, but seems unsure of whether it's to be considered a problem, or if it will continue. They don't seem it's likely to expose them as Uratha, or to endanger them.
  • Wren indicates that their own Pack has some issues being worked through, and it's taken their presence from the Crescent Shadows.
  • Dade fills Wren in about Sister Cathwulf, Lupus et Fidelis, Gregory. Wren seems prone to a more violent solution. Paca suggests trying to bribe someone from the police to take care of the Sister; Dade points out that they lack a police contact and sufficient money to incentivize one.
  • Paca confesses to having been in contact with the Night Watch. She indicates that they seem disorganized, not well-informed, and recently had a "rat" situation at their HQ. She believes they may be able to help the Pack with their Lupus et Fidelis problem. After getting the Pack's consensus, she texts the Night Watch, indicates that she has a lead, and asks when they can meet.
  • Clara confesses that she recently discovered her brother Alex is Wolf-Blooded, and that she's conflicted about whether or not to tell him. The Pack resolves to contact Gregory Whistler, as he has several of them in his employ, to get a better idea of what to expect so that Clara can be prepared to answer questions when she does tell him.
  • Wren confesses to having had a recent unsettling conversation with some Pure, among them Julian Graves, that develops into a rant about his rather Darwinian view of existence.
  • In the midst of the rant, Paca receives a text back from Rafeeq Jackson and says they have a lead as well. The two resolve to meet on Tuesday to exchange information. The gathering is apparently to be something of a celebration of sorts that the cell will fill her in on later.
  • Wren suddenly senses that someone from their Pack has died, and falls to the ground, then shortly after stands back up in a rage and marches out of HQ, snarling that someone is "about to have their legs ripped off and be beaten to death with them." Paca gives a half-hearted "Good luck." Wren responds brightly by saying "Thanks!" before shifting to Urshul form and continuing on. After a moment, the rest of the Pack pursues them.
  • Along the way, Wren picks up the scent of human and Uratha blood, and comes across bullet marks in nearby brickwork. They find the beheaded corpse of LeShaun White, riddled with silver bullets.
    • They begin frantically texting other members of their Pack to check in, who respond that they're alright and sensed the same thing that Wren did. They tell their Pack that LeShaun White is dead, and are immediately inundated with questions about what happened. They send their location, asking them to come quickly.
    • The other members of the Cadillac Company arrive imminently, and seem thirsty for blood even as they review the scene.
    • Bridget Byreen, an Elodoth and second-in-command, questions Wren about their whereabouts at the time of LeShaun White's death. Wren confesses to being with the Crescent Shadows, with the intent of spying on them at the request of a friend. Bridget basically accuses them of consorting with the enemy. Samuel Hanselman attempts to pacify her, but to no avail: she turns Wren out of the Cadillac Company, and in a daze, they walk away.
  • Dade and Clara both expend a Willpower (putting Dade at 5) and they and Paca attempt to remain hidden from the Cadillac Company. They succeed, as the other Uratha are too engrossed in the scene to notice them, and flee unnoticed.
  • Wren senses that the Cadillac Company is performing the Siskur-Dah, presumably to hunt down the killer of LeShaun White, but does not follow, resulting in a Breaking Point.

Thoughts
